The muscles that connect around the center top of the sternum and near the back of the skull are primarily the sternocleidomastoid muscles. These are a pair of muscles located in the neck, which play a vital role in the movement of the head.

  1. Sternocleidomastoid (SCM) Muscle: The SCM muscles originate from the manubrium, which is the upper part of the sternum, and the medial portion of the clavicle (collarbone). They then extend diagonally across the neck and insert at the mastoid process of the temporal bone, which is located just behind the ear, and the lateral portion of the occipital bone, which is at the back of the skull.

These muscles are responsible for various head movements including rotation and flexion. When both muscles contract, they flex the neck forward, and when one muscle contracts, it causes the head to tilt towards the same side and rotate towards the opposite side. They are also involved in elevating the sternum, aiding in forced inhalation.
